My Story

The 3 Reasons Browser-beijin Began

Self-introduction

Hello, my name is Michael Kobayashi. I am the founder and Japanese-to-English translator for browser-beijin.com

I was born in California, graduated university in America with a degree in English teaching, and came to Japan in 2009.

In 2019, I founded browser-beijin.com. Why does the world need yet one more translation-services company, you might ask? Read on below, and you will discover that browser-beijin serves a special niche not catered to by any other currently existing translation company.



The Three Reasons for Browser-beijin:

Reason 1: Eikaiwa is an overkill (sometimes)!

When I came to Japan in 2001 and began teaching English, many of my students were studying "Business English." There were a handful of them who actually needed speaking proficiency for things like telephone conferences and business trips abroad. However, I noticed that a large number of the students would often bring in English documents for checking or translation:

Eikaiwa was clearly an overkill for many students. They were paying nearly 5,000 yen for 40-minutes of English "conversation," then actually using only a few minutes of the classtime for English writing correction.

I thought, "Isn't there a better option for these kind of people--who simply want their English documents corrected?"


Reason 2: Writing email is hard!

After many years in the teaching industry, I wanted to move into translating. As I began to search for jobs, I received some job offers. Hence, I was faced with the necessity of writing response emails in Japanese. In doing so, I found I had a lot of questions about the subtle differences in Japanese phrases, and often did not know the most natural way to say something:

I had no way to know the nuance of various phrases without asking a native speaker. It troubled me that I could not write an email confidently.

I thought, "Is there not an option in this situation--when you are writing an email in a foreign language, and simply have a question about which phrase is most suitable?"


Reason 3: Translating 1 or 2 sentences is expensive!

Finally, when I began working in the translation industry, I came face-to-face with the realities of tight deadlines and extreme "minimum charges" for translation. We did most of the translation in-house, but sometimes needed to subcontract to another translation company. Many times, the company needed just a sentence or two translated, on a tight schedule, and I was surprised at the lack of affordable, quick options:

I saw money flying out the door of the company for such small-scale translation projects. In addition to the cost being high, the time necessary was often long--even for just one sentence or two.

I thought, "Isn't there a faster, cheaper option for companies that only need a handful of words translated or checked?"


    In summary, 3 things...
  1. Cheap & convenient English document correction.
  2. Fast & reliable advice for writing emails.
  3. Affordable small-scale translation for companies, with a quick turnaround.

These are the 3 things that browser-beijin.com offers. If you are 1) using eikaiwa as a document-correction service, 2) struggling with the nuances of English emailing, or 3) tired of the minimum charges & slow response time of big translation companies, I urge you to give browser-beijin a try!
