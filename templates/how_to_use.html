How to Use

The Browser-beijin system explained...

An all-in-one homescreen

Log in to your account, and your homescreen appears. Depending on the time of day, the translation deadline will be different.

Send a request between the hours of 9:30 and 17:45, and a 10-minute timer begins countdown at the bottom of the screen. When the response comes in, it will be displayed at the bottom of the screen. (If the timer reaches zero with no response, your account will automatically be credited the cost of the translation, plus 50 yen).

Send a request at other times of the day, and it will be uploaded into your "Q and A History" column by the deadline.

Your balance is visible in the upper lefthand corner. You can charge your account in increments of 1,000 5,000 or 10,000 yen over our secure https connection (payment services provided by Stripe, and credit card information is not kept on file)

A complete history of everything you have asked, with responses, is visible in the "Q and A History" column. The contents can be downloaded as an excel file at anytime with the click of a button.



3 Options: English Correction, Translation, and Question

There are 3 types of request you can send--English correction, Translation, and Question. Before submitting each type of request, you will be able to see and confirm the cost of the request. If your balance is sufficient and you would like to continue, clicking "Continue" will send the request.

Requesting an English Correction: 7 yen per word

In an "English Correction" request, you input English text, and the translator checks the spelling and grammar:

Example Request

This is pen.

Example Response

This is a pen.


Requesting a Translation: 11 yen per character

In a "Translation" request, you input Japanese text, and the English translation comes back:

Example Request

reba wo tsuyoku hippate kudasai.

Example Response

Please pull the lever strongly.


Asking a Question: 100 yen per question

In a "Question" request, you input a question about grammar or usage, and an answer comes back:

Example Request

Which is more formal, "append" or "add?"

Example Response

"Append" is more formal, but it is usually used for documents only, e.g. -- Append 2 pages to the report.
